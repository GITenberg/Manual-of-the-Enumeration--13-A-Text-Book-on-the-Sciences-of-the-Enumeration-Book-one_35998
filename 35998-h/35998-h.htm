Arrangement

This book I is intended as a progressive textbook, by which anyone who is totally unfamiliar with the Enumeration may, in a practical way, learn its sciences sufficiently to grasp the many beautiful laws of the Enumeration and apply them.

This will help to rebuild your life and give you almost a new world in which to live.

To assist you in gaining this knowledge more easily, this work has been arranged in three major volumes, called Book I, Book II and Book III.

Book I, is designed to introduce you to the subject matter in the Enumeration, in its more simple forms and in its applications.

Book II, is designed to enable you to analyze others and to help them, thereby increasing your own knowledge and ability to order your life, for "he who teaches is doubly taught."

Book III, enables you, as a teacher or minister of the Enumeration, to know and apply some of the deeper phases of group psychology, and to answer some of the questions of the more educated minds, as to "the hope that is in you."

THE ASPECTS OF PLANETS

Definition 23.—a—Conjunction or Conjunct , is seen when two planets are within three degrees of each other's position in the same sign. Conjunct is considered a good aspect.

—b—A planet is in OPPOSITION to another, when they are half way around the Zodiac from each other. This would be six signs or 180 degrees of the circle of the Zodiac. OPPOSITION is considered an adverse or bad aspect.

—c—A planet is SQUARE to another when they are one-fourth of the way around the Zodiac from each other, or 90 degrees of the circle, because that is the arc that forms a square. This would be three signs of the Zodiac apart or 90 degrees. The SQUARE is considered an adverse or bad aspect.

—d—A planet is TRINE to another when they are one-third of the way around the Zodiac from each other, or 120 degrees apart. This would be four signs of the Zodiac. The TRINE is considered a good aspect.

ASPECTS OF PLANETS

—e—A planet is SEXTILE to another when they are one-sixth of the way around the Zodiac from each other, or 60 degrees apart. This would be two signs of the Zodiac apart. The SEXTILE is considered a good aspect.

—f—A planet is SEMI-SEXTILE to another when they are one-twelfth of the way around the Zodiac from each other, or 30 degrees apart. This would be in the next sign. The SEMI-SEXTILE is considered a good aspect.

—g—These are the major aspects. There are finer degrees, but they have little or no perceptible influence.
